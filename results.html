World population maps provide global information, evidence-based analytics, and knowledge on human presence on the planet. The ultimate goal of these maps is to ensure that every person is mapped and counted within critical operations such as policy making and disasters response. Due to the importance associated with world population maps, the assessment of accuracy and reliability of these datasets is key to inform any users on benefit and limitations deriving from their usage.

This spatial raster dataset depicts the distribution of population, expressed as the number of people per cell. Residential population estimates for target years 1975, 1990, 2000 and 2015 provided by CIESIN GPWv4.10 were disaggregated from census or administrative units to grid cells, informed by the distribution and density of built-up as mapped in the Global Human Settlement Layer (GHSL) global layer per corresponding epoch.

The main purpose of this project is to perform an intercomaparison between population data comes from two different sources; GHS and Worldpop. After performing this intercomparison we are going to validate both maps and check the accuracy. The intercomparison process is done by comparing these two maps within each other. On the other hand the validation is done by comparing the maps with a reference data set.
